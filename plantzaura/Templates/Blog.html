How to Take Care of Your Plants

1. Give Them The Right Amount of Light

Every plant has its own "sun personality." Some love bright direct light like succulents, while others prefer soft shade like ferns. Observe where your plant grows best and avoid sudden light changes.

2. Water Smartly

Overwatering is the easiest way to harm a plant. Check the top layer of soil. If it feels dry, water gently until the soil is moist. Plants like cactus need less water, while leafy plants need more.

3. Use Natural Fertilizers

Organic fertilizers enrich the soil slowly and make plants stronger. Some great natural fertilizers include:

  • Bone Meal: Adds phosphorus for strong roots and better flowering.
  • Cow Dung: Improves soil texture and boosts overall plant growth.
  • Neem Cake: Protects roots from pests while adding nutrients.

4. Repot When Needed

If your plant looks cramped or its roots peek out from the pot holes, it's time for a new home. Choose a pot slightly bigger and add fresh soil.

5. Keep an Eye on Pests

Small insects can harm your plants. Using neem oil spray or mild soap water can help keep them safe.

6. Give Them Fresh Air

Plants love airflow, but avoid placing them in strong wind. A little breeze helps prevent fungus and strengthens stems.
